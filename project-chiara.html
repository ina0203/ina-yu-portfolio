Executive Summary

Strategic IMC plan for US market expansion

This integrated marketing communications plan outlines the strategic framework for Chiara Ferragni Collection's entry into the US footwear market. The campaign centers on a New York flagship store launch (December 24, 2016) with a comprehensive omnichannel approach designed to achieve 15% sales growth within the first operational year.

Strategic Objectives

  • Achieve 15% sales growth in US market
  • Strengthen brand awareness among target demographics
  • Maintain existing customers while attracting new ones
  • Establish offline presence in key fashion capitals (NY, LA, Miami)
  • Penetrate US market via Nordstrom, Saks Fifth Avenue

Campaign Pillars

  • Product Focus: Signature footwear collection with "Flirting" eye motif (70%+ of total sales)
  • Influencer Leverage: Chiara's personal brand equity (6.4M Instagram followers)
  • Luxury Partnerships: Louis Vuitton, Cartier, Dior, YSL collaborations
  • Omnichannel Integration: Digital-to-physical retail activation

Strategic Insights

Key learnings & implications

On Influencer-to-Brand Scaling

Personal brand equity can successfully launch product lines, but sustainable growth requires systematic marketing investment. Social following provides initial awareness; conversion to commercial success demands traditional brand-building infrastructure alongside digital engagement capabilities.

On Market Entry Strategy

New market entry—even with established brand recognition—requires coordinated omnichannel activation. Digital strength must be complemented by physical presence and traditional media investment to capture full market opportunity and build credibility with new consumer segments.

On Budget Allocation

Physical retail investment (in-store + outdoor advertising = 72% of budget) remains essential for luxury brand market entry, even in the digital age. The flagship store serves as both revenue driver and brand legitimization platform for digitally-native brands.

On Partnership Strategy

Strategic alliances with established luxury brands (Louis Vuitton, Cartier, Dior, YSL) provide aspirational positioning and credibility transfer. These partnerships enable newer brands to "borrow" heritage and prestige while maintaining their distinctive identity and market positioning.
