02 Consumer Insights

Understanding the Luxury Handbag Consumer

Primary research conducted with 31 target consumers revealed key behavioral patterns and purchase motivations in the luxury handbag market.

Research Framework

Exploring consumer behavior through comprehensive primary research: Who, What, Where, How, and Why consumers purchase luxury handbags.

Target Consumer Behavior

Most interested in Dior, Herm√®s, Kate Spade, Chlo√©, Louis Vuitton and Chanel, but primarily own Coach, Chanel, and Louis Vuitton products. Most own more than 3 luxury handbags and look for a new one at least once a year.

Purchase Patterns & Spending

Most spend under $1,000, but many spend $1,000-$5,000 for luxury handbags. Purchase decisions influenced by style/design, quality craftsmanship, and investment value.

Business Implications

Dior competes at both ends of the luxury spectrum. Through marketing, educate consumers on style/design, quality, and cost (investment value) of Dior handbags. Expand e-commerce and secondary retailer presence.

Target Persona

Fashion-forward women between 25-40 years old with an average household income of $60k/year. Single, employed, college-educated professionals living in metropolitan areas of the U.S. and Asia.

03 SWOT Analysis

Strategic Assessment

Comprehensive analysis of Dior's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats in the competitive luxury handbag market.

SWOT Framework

Strategic analysis methodology for evaluating Dior's competitive position in the luxury handbag market.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Internal factors including brand heritage (est. 1946), strong consumer demand in China (20 stores), but lacking e-commerce outside EU.

Opportunities & Threats

External factors including e-commerce expansion potential, mobile app development, but facing Chanel's e-commerce launch and secondary market challenges.

Strengths

  • Brand history (est. 1946) with worldwide designer name recognition
  • Strong core service values: heritage, value, experience
  • Communicates with in-store customers very well
  • Variety of products and strong designs (8 bag models)
  • #6 of top 15 luxury brand social media mentions
  • Favorable celebrity endorsements (Jennifer Lawrence, etc.)
  • Strong consumer demand in China (20 stores)

Weaknesses

  • Viewed as pricey (cost is not listed on website)
  • Second time in five years searching for new creative director
  • No e-commerce for leather goods outside EU
  • Ranked #54 out of 100 fashion brands by digital IQ score
  • Dior handbags not on target consumers' radar compared to other product lines

Opportunities

  • Promote small leather goods to introduce consumers to accessories line
  • Translate experiential relationship from in-store to online via e-commerce
  • Add leather goods to Yoox/Net-a-porter
  • Green initiative: trade-in of competitor bags for discount
  • Develop mobile app for handbag videos and POP opportunities

Threats

  • Chanel launching e-commerce in late 2016
  • Secondary retail market: Dior handbags on Bag Borrow or Steal
  • Competitors use both primary and secondary e-commerce channels
  • Luxury brands with mobile apps and POP sales (Chanel, Prada, LV, Gucci)
  • Chanel, Gucci, Herm√®s ranked #1-3 in social mentions vs Dior #6
04 Brand Positioning

Market Position Analysis

Competitive positioning maps analyzing Dior's placement among luxury handbag brands based on style, price, prestige, and product range.

Segmentation & Targeting

Strategic approach to market segmentation and target audience identification for Dior leather goods.

Position Map: Style (Design) + Price

Dior positioned in the high-end luxury, modern design quadrant alongside Herm√®s and Chanel, differentiated from affordable luxury brands like Coach and Michael Kors. Classic to Modern on horizontal axis, Low Luxury to High-End Luxury on vertical axis.

Range of Offerings + Price

Dior occupies the prestige/exclusivity quadrant with limited product range, competing with Herm√®s and Louis Vuitton. Mass Market to Exclusivity on horizontal axis, Lower Prestige to Prestige + Status on vertical axis.

Brand Position Statement

"For fashion-forward women between 25-40 years old with an average household income of $60k/year, Dior offers classically designed handbags with a modern edge that are worth investing in because they are exceptionally well-made." Reasons to believe: Heritage (50+ years), Modern yet timeless design, Hand-crafted Italian leather.

08 Strategic Implications

Key Strategic Insights

Critical findings and recommendations derived from the comprehensive market analysis.

E-Commerce & Pricing Strategy

Price was a factor in our survey‚Äîincluding price will be essential for e-commerce success. Convert current website to leverage e-commerce in Asia and US. Partner with secondary retailers (Yoox/Net-a-porter) to expand handbag availability without diluting brand prestige.

Experience & Sustainability

Experiential relationship between brand and consumers differs in-person vs online‚Äîrequires research for e-commerce success. Green initiative ("return on fashion investment" trade-in program) appeals to millennials and supports sustainability positioning.

09 Key Learnings

Project Takeaways

01

Consumer Research Drives Strategy

Primary research with 31 target consumers revealed the gap between consumer interest (Dior) and actual ownership (competitors), informing our awareness-building approach rather than price-driven tactics. Understanding that most spend under $1,000 but aspire to $1,000-$5,000 purchases shaped our entry-point strategy.

02

Omni-Channel Integration

The experiential relationship Dior excels at in-store must be translated to digital channels without compromising brand prestige. This requires careful UX design, exclusive online experiences, and click-to-collect programs that bridge physical and digital touchpoints.

03

Budget Allocation & ROI

Shifting 36% of budget to digital reflects the target demographic's media consumption (digital natives, 25-40) while maintaining premium touchpoints through events (15%) and quality print placements (30%). The 4.9% budget increase signals investment in future growth channels.
